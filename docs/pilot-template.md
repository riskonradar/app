# Pilot Template

A pilot is a structured trial with a real customer team. It is not a vague free trial. It should prove whether Risk on Radar can solve one reliability workflow well enough that the customer would pay.

## Pilot Definition

One customer team uses Risk on Radar on one bounded reliability workflow for 2-4 weeks, with success criteria agreed before the pilot starts.

## Example Pilot Scope

- Asset: turbofan engine subsystem, wind turbine gearbox, pump system, valve train, battery module, or similar.
- Workflow: evidence-backed FMEA update, failure mode research, root-cause evidence review, or corrective action evidence review.
- Output: suggested failure modes, causes, effects, controls, and citations that an engineer can accept, edit, or reject.

## What The Customer Provides

- Target asset or component family
- Existing FMEA rows if legally shareable
- Known failure modes or incidents
- Public sources they trust
- Internal data only if legal/security posture is clear
- Named reviewer who will give feedback

## What We Provide

- Product access or guided demo
- Evidence-backed suggestions
- Source citations and evidence spans
- Review workflow
- Summary of accepted, edited, rejected, and missing suggestions
- Final feedback call

## Success Criteria

Pick 3-5 before starting:
- Reduces research time by at least 30%
- Finds failure modes or causes the team considers relevant
- Provides evidence good enough for engineering review
- Preserves traceability from suggestion to source
- Produces at least one FMEA update the team would consider using
- Identifies a clear paid use case

## Pilot Commercial Options

### Free Design Partner

Use only if the customer gives strong access, weekly feedback, and permission to use anonymized learnings.

### Paid Pilot

Best if the customer has budget. Even a small paid pilot validates seriousness.

Example structure:
- 2-4 weeks
- EUR 1k-5k pilot fee
- Credited toward annual subscription if they continue

### Extended Paid Pilot

Use when procurement is slow but the technical team is serious.

Example:
- 2-3 months
- Narrow scope
- Clear conversion decision at the end

## Pilot Exit

Every pilot ends with one of:
- Convert to paid subscription
- Extend as paid pilot
- Stop and document why
- Introduce budget owner or broader team

Do not let a pilot end with vague interest.
